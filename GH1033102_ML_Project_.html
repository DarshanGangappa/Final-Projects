Introduction

The target of the dataset is another aspect of the audiobook market, particularly, the likelihood of the customers’ repeat buying. It has customer data of aspects such as listening habits, buying behaviour, activity level and a need of help. Such information is useful for companies that are interested in customer retention, effectiveness of the marketing campaigns, and general UX.

Hence, the evaluation of the provided features which are the total time spent on listening, amount spent on purchases, and frequency of support, the dataset provides an understanding of what motivates the customers to build loyalty. The outcome is to use these findings to create forecast models to detect factors that may help determine whether a customer will return so that specific action can be taken to retain the customers.

This dataset proves to be invaluable for analyzing the audiobooks customer trends as it serves as the basis for evidence-based decision-making in an attempt at improving customer experience and business performance.

Conculation

Several models were investigated in order to predict the binary target variable when examining the Audiobooks dataset. Models like Random Forest and Logistic Regression did fairly well, but Naive Bayes had the best accuracy. This is noteworthy since Naive Bayes is an effective method for this dataset due to its simplicity and assumption of feature independence. Furthermore, Naive Bayes frequently performs better for the minority class when dealing with unbalanced data. The findings suggest that Naive Bayes might be a better model, particularly for quick and accurate predictions that require little adjustment. Additional optimization and feature development may improve performance for additional models as well.
